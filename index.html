Bio

My name is Sam, I know a lot of probability, and I want to teach share this information with others. Probability comes up in everyday life more than you would think. For example, consider the follow scenerio:

You're shopping at the grocery store, have finally found the last item on your shopping list, and are now heading to checkout. When you arive at the checkout area, you're astonished to find that there is a line of seven customers even though the line was empty when you first arrived. You think to yourself, How could this happen? What are the chances that there are seven people in line at this time of day when there was nobody in line previously? It may seem unpredictable, but the number of people who arrive in line at a grocery counter can be modeled by something called a stochastic process.
